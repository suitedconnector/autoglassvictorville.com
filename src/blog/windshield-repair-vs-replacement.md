      <h2>Introduction</h2>
      <p>When your windshield gets damaged, your first question is usually: can it be repaired or does it need to be replaced? The answer depends on several factors, and understanding them can save you money while keeping your vehicle safe.</p>

      <h2>Windshield Repair: When It Works</h2>
      <p>Windshield repair is the most cost-effective solution when the damage is minor. Here's what can typically be repaired:</p>

      <h3>Repairable Damage</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Small chips:</strong> Less than 1 inch in diameter</li>
        <li><strong>Cracks:</strong> Less than 12 inches long</li>
        <li><strong>Star breaks:</strong> Radiating cracks from impact point</li>
        <li><strong>Bull's-eye damage:</strong> Circular chips with cone pattern</li>
        <li><strong>Location:</strong> Outside your direct line of sight (driver's vision area)</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Benefits of Repair</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Cost savings:</strong> Repairs are 70-80% cheaper than replacement</li>
        <li><strong>Speed:</strong> Most repairs take 1-2 hours</li>
        <li><strong>Convenience:</strong> Often done while you wait</li>
        <li><strong>Insurance:</strong> Usually fully covered with low/no deductible</li>
        <li><strong>Preserves glass:</strong> Your original OEM glass remains intact</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Windshield Replacement: When It's Necessary</h2>
      <p>Some damage is too severe to repair. Replacement is necessary in these cases:</p>

      <h3>When Replacement Is Required</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Large cracks:</strong> Longer than 12 inches</li>
        <li><strong>Multiple cracks:</strong> Several cracks in different areas</li>
        <li><strong>Impact in driver's view:</strong> Damage in direct line of sight</li>
        <li><strong>Edge damage:</strong> Cracks near the windshield edges</li>
        <li><strong>Severe damage:</strong> Damage too extensive to safely repair</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Replacement Benefits</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Restores full visibility</li>
        <li>Returns structural integrity</li>
        <li>Eliminates safety concerns</li>
        <li>Improves vehicle appearance</li>
        <li>Provides warranty protection</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>The Repair Process</h2>
      <p>If your windshield can be repaired, here's what to expect:</p>

      <ol>
        <li><strong>Assessment:</strong> Technician evaluates damage location, size, and type</li>
        <li><strong>Cleaning:</strong> Damaged area is thoroughly cleaned</li>
        <li><strong>Injection:</strong> Specialized resin is injected into the damage</li>
        <li><strong>Curing:</strong> Resin is allowed to set (usually 24 hours)</li>
        <li><strong>Finishing:</strong> Excess material is removed and area is polished</li>
      </ol>

      <h2>The Replacement Process</h2>
      <p>If replacement is necessary:</p>

      <ol>
        <li><strong>Removal:</strong> Old windshield is carefully removed</li>
        <li><strong>Frame prep:</strong> Windshield frame is cleaned and prepped</li>
        <li><strong>Installation:</strong> New windshield is installed with automotive-grade adhesive</li>
        <li><strong>Sealing:</strong> Weatherproof seals are applied</li>
        <li><strong>Curing:</strong> Adhesive is allowed to cure (usually 24 hours)</li>
      </ol>

      <h2>Cost Comparison</h2>
      <p>Here's a typical cost breakdown:</p>

      <ul>
        <li><strong>Repair:</strong> $100-$400 (often free with insurance)</li>
        <li><strong>Replacement (aftermarket):</strong> $300-$500</li>
        <li><strong>Replacement (OEM):</strong> $500-$1,200+</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Insurance Coverage</h2>
      <p>Most comprehensive auto insurance policies cover windshield damage with little to no deductible. Check your policy or call your insurance agent to find out:</p>

      <ul>
        <li>What type of damage is covered</li>
        <li>Your deductible amount</li>
        <li>Whether repair or replacement is covered</li>
        <li>If you need to use their preferred vendor</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Don't Wait on Windshield Damage</h2>
      <p>Whether your damage needs repair or replacement, don't put it off. Windshield damage can:</p>

      <ul>
        <li>Spread and worsen quickly</li>
        <li>Create safety hazards</li>
        <li>Reduce structural integrity</li>
        <li>Affect airbag deployment</li>
        <li>Be illegal to drive with in California</li>
      </ul>
